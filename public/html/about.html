Meet Thomas Pritchard

About Thomas

A native Charlestonian, Thomas grew up downtown and spent summers on Sullivan’s Island. He graduated from Porter-Gaud. He attended the University of South Carolina on an Alumni Scholarship and graduated with a B.A. in Political Science in 1989. While in school he worked as a page in the South Carolina House of Representatives for the late Ted Mappus, served as an intern in the Office of the President for the University and was Chief of Staff to the President of the Student Government Association.

Thomas graduated from the University of South Carolina School of Law in 1992 and returned to Charleston, purchasing his first home in Byrnes Down.

Thomas married Elizabeth Condon Pritchard in 1996, and they purchased a home on Betsy’s native James Island in 1998. Together, they raised three wonderful and accomplished children, Sarah (25), Thomas (25) and Harrison (21) in the home where Betsy and Thomas still reside.

Career

After graduating from the University of South Carolina School of Law in 1992, Thomas returned to Charleston and had the honor and privilege of spending a year serving as law clerk to the late Honorable William L. Howard, Sr., Resident Judge, Ninth Judicial Circuit. Thereafter, he became an associate at Barnwell, Whaley, Patterson & Helms, eventually rising to the rank of partner. In 2006, he established the firm of Pritchard & Elliott, with his brother, Edward, and close friend and partner at Barnwell, Whaley, James Elliott. He remained with Pritchard & Elliott, and its successor, Pritchard Law Group, until 2017, when he set out on a new adventure with Parker Nelson & Associates.

In 2022, Thomas became the managing attorney for Vernis & Bowling of South Carolina, a Florida-based firm with eighteen offices in six states. He opened a Charleston office on Savannah Highway in West Ashley. He manages a very active insurance defense and worker’s compensation defense practice throughout the State and is excited about the growth trajectory of the firm in South Carolina.

He has been a certified circuit court mediator since 2011. He is admitted to practice in the State of South Carolina (1992), United States District Court for the District of South Carolina (1993), and the United States Court of Appeals for the Fourth Circuit (1994). He belongs to the Charleston County Bar, the South Carolina Bar, the Claims and Legal Management Alliance, and the National Retail & Restaurant Defense Association.

Political Experience

In 1999, Thomas felt it was time to start giving back to a community that had given him and so many generations of his family before him such incredible opportunities and enjoyment. He was first elected to the Board of Commissioners for the City of Charleston Commissioner of Public Works (CPW or CWS) in November of 1999, to serve a six-year term commencing in January of 2000. Since that time, he has had both the pleasure and privilege of serving the citizens of Charleston and the greater Lowcountry region for almost twenty-four years. He has been Chairman of the Board since 2006, which has afforded him the opportunity to work with an incredible team of managers and associates as well as fellow commissioners.

Since Thomas joined the Board, CWS has established or renewed long-term water and/or sanitary sewer service contracts with Mt. Pleasant, North Charleston, St. John’s Water, Folly Beach, James Island PSD, and many others. The operation of CWS truly takes both a regional vision and regional cooperation.

Proven, responsible leadership.

In his time there, Charleston Water has received two bond rating increases, being upgraded from AA to AA+ in 2001 and from AA+ to AAA in 2009, maintaining the AAA rating through fourteen consecutive ratings reviews. Charleston Water is one of only a handful of combined water and wastewater utilities in the County to enjoy an AAA rating. This results in significant cost saving to the rate payors when Charleston Water goes to the bond market to borrow to fund capital projects.

Thomas has spurned a number of opportunities and entreaties from other community leaders over the years to seek higher office. He feels strongly that he can best continue to serve the citizens of Charleston and the Lowcountry by continuing to apply his knowledge and expertise to service on the Board of CWS.
